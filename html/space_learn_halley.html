Frequent Visitor


Halley's Comet is a comet visible from Earth every 75-76 years.

Next Sighting


The last time Halley's Comet was visible was in 1986 and is predicted to appear again in 2061.

Observed by spacecraft


It was the first comet to be observed by spacecraft that analysed it in 1986 using the Giotto probe.

Twice in a lifetime


Halley's Comet is the only naked eye comet that can appear twice in a human lifetime.

Comet's namesake


The comet is named after Edmond Halley, who in 1705, using historical data first identified the comet.

An omen?


It appeared in 1066 and was considered an omen before the battle of Hastings; it is portrayed on the Bayeux Tapestry.
