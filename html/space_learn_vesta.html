Second biggest


Vesta is considered the second largest asteroid by both mass and volume after the dwarf planet Ceres.

Name Origin


Vesta is named after the Roman goddess Vesta, the virgin goddess of home and hearth, the namesake of the vestal virgins.

Shining Bright


Vesta is the brightest asteroid visible from Earth and, at times, it can be faintly visible to the naked eye.

Almost a dwarf planet


Vesta which is almost spherical was nearly classified as an dwarf planet but did not made it in.

Being well-known


Vesta is the origin of many meteorites that have been found on Earth making its composition to be fairly well known.

The many troughts


An extensive system of troughs encircles Vesta's equatorial region. The largest, named Divalia Fossa, is bigger than the Grand Canyon.
